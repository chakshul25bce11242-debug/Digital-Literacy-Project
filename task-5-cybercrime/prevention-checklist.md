# Stay Safe Online: Identity Theft Prevention Checklist

1. Use strong and unique passwords for all important accounts such as email, banking, and social media.
2. Enable two-factor authentication wherever possible to add extra security to your accounts.
3. Never share OTPs, passwords, PINs, or banking details with anyone, even if they claim to be from a trusted company.
4. Do not click on suspicious links sent through SMS, email, WhatsApp, or social media platforms.
5. Avoid uploading sensitive personal documents like Aadhaar, PAN, bank passbook, or ID cards on untrusted websites.
6. Check your social media privacy settings and avoid sharing too much personal information publicly.
7. Always verify calls, emails, or messages that ask for personal, academic, or financial details.
8. Use only secure and trusted websites while logging in, making payments, or submitting important information.
9. Regularly monitor your bank account, email, and other online accounts for any unusual activity.
10. For UPI safety, verify the receiver’s name before sending money and never approve unknown payment requests.
11. Do not scan random QR codes to receive money, because scanning a QR code is usually meant for making a payment.
12. If identity theft or online fraud occurs, report it immediately on the National Cyber Crime Portal at cybercrime.gov.in and call the helpline number 1930.
