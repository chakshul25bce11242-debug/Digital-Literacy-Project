# Case Study: Identity Theft

Identity theft is a cybercrime in which a criminal steals another person’s personal information and uses it without permission for fraudulent purposes. This information may include the victim’s full name, phone number, Aadhaar number, bank details, email credentials, passwords, or social media account information. Such crimes usually happen when people unknowingly share sensitive details on fake websites, suspicious links, untrusted apps, or public networks.

A common example of identity theft can involve a college student receiving a fake message that appears to come from a bank, shopping website, or scholarship portal. The message may ask the student to click a link and verify personal details. Once the student enters information such as login credentials, OTPs, or card details, the fraudster captures the data. After that, the criminal may misuse the identity to access bank accounts, create fake online profiles, make unauthorized purchases, or send messages pretending to be the victim.

Students, job seekers, and frequent internet users are often targeted because they regularly use digital platforms and may not always verify suspicious requests carefully. The consequences of identity theft can be serious, including financial loss, account misuse, damage to personal reputation, and emotional stress.

This case study shows that sharing personal information carelessly online can lead to major problems. It highlights the importance of digital awareness, privacy protection, and cautious online behaviour to prevent identity theft.
